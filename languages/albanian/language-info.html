General Information

Albanian is an Indo-European language spoken by the Albanians in the Balkans and the Albanian diaspora. It compromises an independent branch within the Indo-European languages and isn't closely related to any other language. Modern historians and linguists believe that the language may have descended from a southern Illyrian dialect

Fun Facts

  • The Albanian diaspora (number of Albanians outside the native country) is huge. Albania has a population of a little under 3 million people but it's estimated that there are around 7 million Albanian speakers across the planet
  • Albanian is an isolated language, taking up a branch of its own on the Indo-European language tree (has no close language relatives)
  • Albanian has a confusing history and it is not entirely clear how the language evolved. It has no surviving relatives and its ancestors are obscure
  • Many dialects of the language exist, but there are two main dialects, Tosk and Gheg. Across Albanian, they are separated by a north-south divide with Tosk spoken in southern Albania and Gheg in northern Albania (similarly for surrounding regions)
  • The approximate border for the two Albanian dialects is the Shkumbin River
  • The standardized version of Albanian uses the Tosk dialect, with around 80% of the official language being Tosk
  • The Albanian language has has multiple alphabets over the years, even consisting of the Arabic alphabet and at another time, the Cyrillic alphabet
